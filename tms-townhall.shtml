TMS Town Hall Meeting (February 17, 2016)

The next meeting in the “Rise of Data in Materials Reseach” will be held during the 2016 TMS Annual Meeting in Nashville, TN.

Who is organizing?
Ellad B. Tadmor and Ryan S. Elliott (University of Minnesota). Funding provided by the National Science Foundation (NSF) and The Minerals, Metals and Materials Society (TMS).

Who should attend?
This town hall meeting is free and open to anyone (whether registered for the TMS meeting or not) who is interested in expressing an opinion on the role of data in materials research.

What is this about?
As explained in the Background section, materials research is in a state of flux as it grapples with how to address the technical, ethical, and philosophical issues associated with the increasing prominence of data in materials research. These topics were discussed by work groups at a 2-day workshop at UMD in June 2015. The recommendations from these groups will be presented at this meeting and opened for public debate. The meeting will be recorded and the feedback obtained will be integrated into a report being compiled to accurately reflect our communityâ€™s perspective on the role of data in materials research. You can also comment now on our discussion forum.

When and where is the meeting?
The meeting will be held on Wednesday February 17, 2016 at the 12:00pm at the Omni Nashville Hotel, which serves as the TMS headquarters hotel, in Nashville, TN across the street from the Nashville Convention Center.

How to register?
The meeting is free and will include lunch. We will need a head count ahead of the event, we therefore request that you register in advance. Information on how to register will be posted soon.
